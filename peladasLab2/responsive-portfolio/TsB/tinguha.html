About the Creator

Tinguha sa Bulan is a solo project developed as part of the Special Problem course, a thesis-equivalent requirement in the Computer Science program at UP Cebu. Beyond fulfilling academic requirements, this project was driven by a personal concern—the fading presence of Cebuano oral literature in today’s modern and globalized world. To help address this, the game reimagines the traditional Visayan myth of the Bakunawa and the Seven Moons into a gamified, interactive story aimed at modern audiences, bridging the gap between heritage and technology.

About the Game

Tinguha sa Bulan, depending on the English translation, carries two meanings—Desire of the Moon and Desire for the Moon. In this retelling, why not both?


Desire of the Moon: The moon longs to be with its dear friend, the Bakunawa, to escape the burden of being the light for everyone.

Desire for the Moon: The Bakunawa yearns for the day the moon can join it in the depths, away from duty, simply to be together again.


They do not seek to harm, only to be with one another. They just wanted each other to be happy.



Tinguha sa Bulan is a short AR (Augmented Reality) game that reimagines the traditional Visayan legend of the Bakunawa and the Seven Moons. This project explores how technology—specifically AR—can serve as a bridge between oral tradition and modern digital storytelling.

My Contribution to the Game

As a solo developer, I created Tinguha sa Bulan, an augmented reality (AR) mobile game that reimagines the Cebuano myth of the Bakunawa and the Seven Moons. The project blends digital storytelling with real-world interaction to promote and preserve local oral traditions through gameplay.

Game Design & Concept

Designed the game from the ground up, focusing on integrating traditional Cebuano mythology into a modern AR experience. The game revolves around collecting scattered moons as the Bakunawa to unlock chapters of the reimagined story.

Programming & Gameplay

Programmed the core mechanics using C# in Unity, enabling players to control a 3D Bakunawa in real-world spaces. Implemented AR interactions, time-based challenges, and narrative progression systems.

Research & Collaboration

Consulted a literature lecturer from UP Cebu to ensure the retelling stayed rooted in Cebuano culture and the original Bakunawa myth, enriching the story's authenticity.

Visual Art & UI Design

Illustrated and polished game assets, including backgrounds, UI elements, and animations, using Krita and Aseprite. Created wireframes in Figma to map out intuitive screen layouts and user flow.

Development Process

Followed an iterative waterfall model to structure development stages, allowing flexibility for refinement and improvement throughout the design, implementation, and testing phases.
