About the Game

In a world where cats of all types—mages, swordsmen, knights, and rogues—are captured and forced into a brutal arena, only the deadliest can claim victory. These skilled cats must rely on their unique abilities and ruthless instincts to eliminate every rival standing in their way. Each battle is a savage fight for dominance, and only the fiercest feline will emerge victorious, while the rest are doomed to a grim fate: being turned into siomeow. Siomeow Legends is a top-down, free-for-all deathmatch game where survival isn’t enough—players must dominate the battlefield by defeating as many opponents as possible to secure the top spot. Set in randomized arenas filled with hidden dangers and cunning traps, players control fierce feline champions, fighting tooth and claw to rack up kills and climb the leaderboard.

My Contribution to the Game

As part of a five-person team, I contributed to the development of Siomeow Legends, a fast-paced, cat-themed game made in Unity. My responsibilities centered on front-end development, game art direction, and gameplay interface programming.

Front-End Development

Designed and implemented UI elements such as buttons, menus, and the post-game ranking screen using Unity and C#. Ensured responsive scene transitions and intuitive navigation throughout the game.

Art Direction & Visual Design

Illustrated key backgrounds including the home screen and other major scenes using Krita and Aseprite. Directed the game's overall visual style and color palette to maintain a consistent, playful aesthetic.

Animation

Refined the player character animations, ensuring smooth transitions for cat characters equipped with various weapons. Adjusted motion timing and visual feedback to enhance clarity during gameplay.

Game Programming

Utilized C# in Unity to code gameplay-related front-end systems, including ranking logic and scene handling. Contributed to game functionality through collaborative feature development.

Agile Collaboration

Followed a SCRUM-based agile workflow, participating in sprint cycles to review builds, prioritize tasks, and iterate based on playtesting feedback and peer review.
